# Structured Information Field Ontology (SIFO)

*This document defines the minimal ontology implied by Structured Information Field Dynamics (SIFD), strictly operational and consistent with the field model. All terms refer to measurable structures and dynamics; interpretive concepts like awareness are noted as conceptual tools, not additional entities.*

---

## Human-Readable Summary

SIFD describes reality as emerging from a single universal field ```Ψ(x^μ)```.  
Everything we observe—matter, forces, and measurable processes—is generated by **stable, recurrent, localized structures** in this field.  
Observers are formalized as **localized perspectives** of ```Ψ```; they operationally measure patterns of distinctions without introducing external entities.  
The information functional ```I[Ψ]``` quantifies distinctions, whose gradients give rise to forces and interactions, including gravity and quantum-like effects.  
SIFO is fully operational, mathematically grounded, and directly complements SIFD, translating its field dynamics into a consistent ontology.
